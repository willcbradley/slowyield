What Is Life?

We are alive.

Our bodies are unbelievably complex machines, with unbelievably complex emergent properties. We can run, and jump, and laugh, and cry, and question the meaning of our own existence.

But how are we alive? What does it mean to be alive?

There's no single definition, but we can describe lots of characteristics. Maybe the most notable of these is that life resists entropy. It fights against changes in the environment, and tries to maintain a stable internal state.

Biochemistry is the study of how such life can arise from lifeless matter.

It'd take a lifetime to master, so we'll only touch on the more foundational, timeless principles in these pages.

I hope you find it as useful as I do.
